Perfect Mask

The aim of the Perfect Mask project was to create two perfect layer masks from black-and-white images from the 1950s, 1960s, or 1970s, resembling the work of John Baldessari. In other words, we had to create one work from a previously conceived image by covering a figure or object in the foreground with a solid color to create a stark contrast between the subject and the background and vice versa.

We used Adobe Photoshop to manipulate the subject matter of the images to control what the audience could see. We used layer masks, the paintbrush tool, the paint bucket tool, and the zoom tool intermittently to block out the figure or object in the first image and the background in the second image.

Original Images

I thoroughly enjoyed this project. I spent a lot of time picking out the photos I wanted to use. The first one, with the red woman and the gawking men, drew me in because I thought about the perspective of the viewer and the men and the woman and how manipulating what the audience perceives can enhance the weight of the photo. I specifically used a deep red to evoke feelings of seduction or danger, the two different perspectives experienced in that scene. The second photo was of a woman with iguanas on her head. I started looking for photos with Mexican content because I wanted to explore my Hispanic culture and pay homage to it through this project, but what really caught my eye was the somber character of the woman versus the absurd, yet gorgeous crown of iguanas. Zooming in, I was able to achieve sharp, clean detail.
