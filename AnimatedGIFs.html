Animated GIFs

For the Animated GIFs project, we created a two to five-second looped frame animation using photos or illustrations of our choice to make a never-ending video sequence.

We used the video timeline tool in Adobe Photoshop to create the animation. I used linear motion in one of my GIFs and rotational movement in the other, allowing me to practice a variety of approaches. In this project, we were introduced to a new exporting method using Save for Web (Legacy) so that we could upload our work to the web and view it from there.

Technical Notes

  • Adobe Photoshop
  • Paintbrush Tool
  • Layer Masking
  • Timeline Tool
  • Linear Motion
  • Rotational Motion

I had the idea of creating a loop of two boats chasing each other to no end, and the best way to accomplish that was through radial motion. I found an image of a boat turning in the water and duplicated it to create an implied circle. I made the food tray animation for extra credit and to continue exploring the different ways I could loop images to create the illusion of video. I used linear motion to make the trays seem as though they disappeared off the page, and a continuous supply of trays proceeded behind them. When saving them for the web, I adjusted the color limits and sizes to portray different aesthetics. That choice created four very different products.
