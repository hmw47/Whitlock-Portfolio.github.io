Bad Photoshop

“Bad Photoshop” was a project meant to challenge yourself by embracing imperfection. The objective was to create three composite images from self-made contemporary still-lifes, influenced by Lucas Blalock.

We were instructed to use Adobe Photoshop and create compositions that blur the lines between art and imperfection by exploring functions and revealing the tools of creation. We specifically had to emphasize the use of the eraser tool, layer masking, clone stamp tool, selection tools, and blur filters.

Orginal Images

I am personally an imperfectionist when it comes to art. I have even been tasked with practicing to draw with my left hand to accept faults and flaws in my work. So, this project was especially challenging because it went against my nature to make something that wasn’t “correct”. In fact, this project was probably one of the most time-consuming projects this semester, because of how many times I had to start over. At some point, I had to just accept the fact that the result was meant to be chaotic. I focused on staging my still lifes and adding features digitally, creating a veil between what's really there and what appeared virtually. I was able to achieve this by using a variety of tools and making it obviously chalant.
