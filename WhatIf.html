What If

“What If” was an extensive project that encouraged us, as students, to compose imagery based on a narrative foundation. We were asked to choose from three speculative fiction themes to build our project around. We researched our themes and curated images to base two symbolic token designs on. This project was all about design.

We used Adobe Illustrator to create the two tokens that represent the narrative you fabricated. I personally used Pinterest to assemble my resources and Adobe Photoshop to edit them before creating the tokens on Adobe Illustrator.


Narrative

Written in Stone

The cold wind bit Blair’s skin, whipping the loose strands of her hair across her face. The damp air wrapped around the hills, making the tops look like little islands in a sea of clouds. Blair had just hiked high enough to clear the fog when she reached the dig site. The team usually started around seven in the morning, but after a restless night, Blair was eager to hike up to Clava Cairns and begin working.

Each student in the doctorate program for Archaeology at Harvard University was assigned a location as a project to gain experience in the field, and the spot at Clava Cairns was designated for Blair. Of course, she knew these structures were significant, but to what extent? What did archaeologists and anthropologists actually know for certain about them, what breakthroughs were being made, and what evidence was written in stone?

Unbuttoning her collar, something caught her eye. There were fresh bare feet printed into the soft dirt around the structure. Slowly, Blair took a cautious step toward the footprints. They disappeared around the bend and into the bush. Without hesitation, she followed them, like another force pulled her onto its path. They led her further up the hill, almost a half-mile, and into a clearing. What stood there took her breath away. In the center of the clearing rose an enormous stone formation, painted red by the rising sun. The fading full moon nested in the center, framed by two tilted pillars.

The world turned silent. Even the wind ceased its persistent howl. Blair made her way to the center of the stone circle. Just then, a sweet melody broke the silence.

“The Song Thrush sings again,”.

Whisking around, Blair turned and found the culprit for the prints. There stood a small, elderly woman, barefoot with nothing but a shift on, hair glowing golden against the sunrise. Blair opened her mouth to speak, but, stunned, nothing came out.

“The woman of the moon has returned. Welcome back, my dear,” the old woman smiled.

“I’m sorry…. What?” Blair finds her voice in utter confusion.

“Why you have brought the moon back into rhythm,” the old woman answered. Noticing Blair’s bewilderment, she added, “From the night the moon disappeared, of course. The night the ring of fire was formed and the seas sank into the deep chasms. You have been reborn to steady the tides and bring balance to the world,”.

“The day the moon blinked? That’s just a myth,” gathering her senses, she added, “Come down this path with me and I’ll get you a blanket and a hot cup of coffee,”.

“It’s only a myth to those who don’t believe. Don’t worry, darling. Your memory will come back soon,” the old woman said, stepping forward and grabbing Blair’s hand.

She gently pulled Blair around to the stone she had her back to. The moon barely hovered over the top of it, now. There it was, right in front of her, written in stone.

Narrative Inspiration


There were two specific techniques I used that legitimized the designs. Typing text in the round and using a clipping mask to round off images were two methods I utilized to create a more refined token. The only thing I had a hard time with was narrowing down my ideas, so I just ended up making four tokens instead of two and submitting my two least favorites as extras for fun. I definitely took advantage of this project. I wanted to make the best images I could, but I also just wanted to create something that I loved while exploring Adobe Illustrator. I’m especially proud of the use of my knowledge in Adobe Photoshop from previous assignments to attain the desired results.
