Glitch and Grit

“Glitch and Grit” was a project designed to introduce us to HTML. We were tasked with developing a three-page HTML site that considers the communicative power that low-resolution, compressed, or distorted images hold. We were specifically meant to embody imperfection and disruption through our work.

I used the Phoenix IDE to build my site. I referenced W3Schools to learn how to code.

Technical Notes

  • Phoenix IDE
  • W3Schools
  • Adobe Photoshop
  • Resizing Tool
  • Github Pages

This assignment took me the longest out of all the projects this semester. The main issue I faced was the organization of folders and importing them to the Phoenix IDE to use in my site. The thing that stumped me for days was a minor validity issue in the name of my file. A singular character made the entire file unreadable, and after trying everything under the sun to troubleshoot my issue, simply renaming the file was the resolution. Again, attention to detail is crucial in digital media. Other than that, I really enjoyed finding the patterns in coding and finding different ways to add variety to my content to make it more visually appealing.
